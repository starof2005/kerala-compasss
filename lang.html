Kerala Travel Helper: Language and Culture Guide

Common Greetings and Basic Phrases

  • Hello: നമസ്കാരം (Namaskāram)
  • Good Morning: സുപ്രഭാതം (Suprabhātam)
  • Good Night: ശുഭ രാത്രി (Shubha Rāthri)
  • Thank you: നന്ദി (Nandi)
  • Welcome: സ്വാഗതം (Swāgatham)
  • How are you?: സുഖമാണോ? (Sukhamaano?)
  • I am fine: സുഖമാണു (Sukhamaanu)
  • What is your name?: നിന്റെ പേര് എന്താണ്? (Ninte Pēru Enthānu?)
  • My name is…: എന്റെ പേര് ... ആണ് (Ente Pēru ... ānu)
  • Nice to meet you: നിന്നെ കണ്ടതിൽ സന്തോഷം (Ninne Kandaṭhil Santhōsham)
  • Yes: ആം (Aam)
  • No: ഇല്ല (Illa)
  • Please: ദയവായി (Dayavāyi)
  • Sorry: ക്ഷമിക്കണം (Kṣhamikkaṇam)
  • Excuse me: ക്ഷമിക്കണം (Kṣhamikkaṇam)
  • Goodbye: വിട (Viḍa)
  • See you soon: ഉടൻ കാണാം (Uḍan Kāṇām)
  • Take care: ശ്രദ്ധിക്കൂ (Shradhikkū)
  • I love you: ഞാൻ നിന്നെ സ്നേഹിക്കുന്നു (Ñān Ninne Snehikkunnu)
  • I miss you: ഞാൻ നിന്നെ ഓർക്കുന്നു (Ñān Ninne Ōrkunnu)

Numbers (Basic)

  • Zero: പൂജ്യം (Pūjyam)
  • One: ഒന്ന് (Onnu)
  • Two: രണ്ട് (Randu)
  • Three: മൂന്ന് (Moonnu)
  • Four: നാല് (Naalu)
  • Five: അഞ്ച് (Anchu)
  • Six: ആറ് (Aaru)
  • Seven: ഏഴ് (Ezh)
  • Eight: എട്ട് (Ettu)
  • Nine: ഒന്‍പത് (Onpathu)
  • Ten: പത്ത് (Pathu)

Transportation

  • Bus: ബസ് (Bas)
  • Train: ട്രെയിൻ (Train)
  • Taxi: ടാക്സി (Tāksi)
  • Where is the bus stop?: ബസ് സ്റ്റോപ്പ് എവിടെയാണ്? (Bas Stop Evidēyaanu?)
  • I need a ticket to…: എനിക്ക് ... ൽ ഒരു ടിക്കറ്റ് വേണം (Enikku ...il Oru Ticket Vēṇam)
  • When is the next bus/train?: അടുത്ത ബസ്/ട്രെയിൻ എപ്പോഴാണ്? (Adutha Bas/Train Eppozhaanu?)
  • How much is the fare?: കൂലി എത്രയാണ്? (Koōli Ethrayānu?)
  • I want to go to…: എനിക്ക് ... ൽ പോകണം (Enikku ...il Pōkaṇam)

Emergency Phrases

  • Help!: സഹായിക്കൂ! (Sahāyikkū!)
  • Call the police: പോലീസ് വിളിക്കൂ (Polīce Viḷikkū)
  • I am lost: ഞാൻ വഴിതെറ്റി (Ñān Vaḻithēṛṭi)
  • I need a doctor: എനിക്ക് ഡോക്ടർ വേണം (Enikku Dōkṭar Vēṇam)
  • Fire: തീ (Thī)
  • Accident: അപകടം (Apakaṭam)

Dining and Accommodation

  • I am hungry: എനിക്ക് വിശക്കുന്നുണ്ട് (Enikku Vishakunnuṇṭu)
  • Where is a good restaurant?: നല്ല ഹോട്ടൽ എവിടെയാണ്? (Nalla Hoṭṭel Evidēyaanu?)
  • Vegetarian food: സസ്യാഹാരഭക്ഷണം (Sasyaahaarabhakṣhaṇam)
  • Non-vegetarian food: മാംസഭക്ഷണം (Maamsabhakṣhaṇam)
  • Water: വെള്ളം (Veḷḷam)

Shopping

  • How much does this cost?: ഇതിന്റെ വില എത്രയാണ്? (Ethinte Vila Ethrayānu?)
  • Can you give a discount?: വില കുറയ്ക്കാമോ? (Vila Kuṛaykkāmo?)
  • I want to buy this: ഇത് എനിക്ക് വേണം (Ithu Enikku Vēṇam)

Tourist Phrases

  • Where is this place?: ഈ സ്ഥലം എവിടെയാണ്? (Ee Sthalam Evidēyaanu?)
  • Can you take a photo?: ഒരു ഫോട്ടോ എടുക്കാമോ? (Oru Photo Edukkāmo?)
  • Beautiful: സുന്ദരം (Sundaram)

Major Festivals:

  • Onam: Harvest festival with boat races and the grand Onam Sadya (feast).
  • Vishu: Malayalam New Year, marked by the Vishu Kani ritual.
  • Thiruvathira: A festival celebrated by women with traditional dances.

ONAM

Kerala’s harvest festival, celebrated with grand feasts (Onasadya), floral decorations (Pookalam), Vallamkali (boat races), and traditional games..

THRISSUR POORAM

A vibrant temple festival featuring caparisoned elephants, traditional percussion ensembles, and an awe-inspiring fireworks display.

VISHU

The Malayalam New Year, marked by Vishukkani (auspicious sight), fireworks, and feasts.

ATTUKAL PONGALA

A unique festival where millions of women gather to prepare offerings to the goddess Attukal Bhagavathy.

NEHRU TROPHY BOAT RACE

A popular Vallamkali (snake boat race) held in the Punnamada Lake in Alappuzha.

MAKARAVILAKKU

Celebrated at Sabarimala, marking the celestial light seen on Makara Sankranti.

KALPATHI RATOLSAVAM

A chariot festival in Palakkad, featuring processions of decorated temple chariots.

BAKRID AND EID

Showcasing Kerala’s communal harmony, these Islamic festivals are celebrated with prayers and feasts.

CHRISTMAS

A festive season observed with midnight masses, carols, and traditional Kerala Christmas dishes.

DIWALI

Although less prominent, Diwali is celebrated with lights, sweets, and fireworks.

Kerala's cultural richness is reflected in its art forms:

KATHAKALI

Traditional dance drama with expressive performances.

MOHINIYATTAM

Graceful classical dance performed by women.

THEYYAM

Traditional ritual art with vibrant costumes and makeup.

KALARIPAYATTU

Ancient martial art form involving physical postures and movements.

CHAKYAR KOOTHU

A traditional satirical art form performed in temples, mime, and humor.

OTTAMTHULLAL

A poetic performance combining dance and music

PANCHAVAADHYAM

A percussion ensemble of five traditional instruments, creating a rhythmic musical experience.

Delicious Kerala Cuisine

POROTTA BEEF

A popular Kerala dish with flaky porotta served with spicy beef curry.

PAZHAMPOZHI

Traditional Kerala banana fritters made with ripe bananas and a crispy outer layer.

KAPPA MEEN CURRY

A classic Kerala combo of boiled tapioca (kappa) and flavorful fish curry (meen curry).

PAZHAMKANJI

Rice porridge served with mashed banana, a comforting dish popular for breakfast.

CHATTI CHOORU

A traditional Kerala meal served in a clay pot, usually with rice and fish curry.

PUTTU AND KADALA

Steamed rice cake served with black chickpea curry, a popular breakfast dish.

APPAM AND STEW

Soft, lace-edged appam paired with coconut milk-based stew made with chicken or vegetables.

THALASSERY BIRYANI

A flavorful and aromatic biryani from North Kerala, made with small-grain rice and spices.

MALABAR PAROTTA

Soft and flaky layered flatbread, a must-have with Kerala-style curries.

KERALA SADHYA

A grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf, with a variety of dishes and payasam.

Traditional Clothing: Mundu (Traditional Dhoti), Kasavu Sari.

Heritage Practices: Ayurveda, martial arts like Kalaripayattu, Handicrafts like coir and bamboo work.
